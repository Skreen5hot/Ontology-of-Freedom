## Reading Strategies by Expertise

### For Philosophy of Mind Specialists

**Focus on:**
- AAT Paper 1, Sections III-IV (axioms and Triple-I derivation)
- Critical Review, Part I (Consciousness Assessment)
- The phenomenal necessity debate and functional mattering argument

**Key questions for you:**
- Does the structural account of "mattering" succeed?
- Are the phenomenological objections adequately addressed?
- Is the move from consciousness to structure philosophically legitimate?

---

### For AI Ethics Specialists

**Focus on:**
- AAT Paper 2, Sections II, IV (case studies and governance)
- Critical Review, Part II (Answerability Framework)
- IRB model and tiered protections

**Key questions for you:**
- Is the governance framework implementable?
- Does it align with existing AI ethics literature?
- What are the institutional barriers to adoption?

---

### For Legal/Governance Specialists

**Focus on:**
- Critical Review, Part II (Answerability Framework)
- AAT Paper 2, Section IV (legal frameworks)
- Precedents: corporate personhood, trust law, environmental standing

**Key questions for you:**
- Does the answerability stack prevent moral laundering?
- Is the legal framework proposal realistic?
- What modifications would be needed for legal adoption?
